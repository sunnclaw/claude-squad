# Dental Clinic Outreach Playbook

This playbook supports `CMP-10` and gives the founder a consistent script set for dental missed-call recovery discovery.

## Core positioning

- We help private dental clinics recover missed-call revenue without adding front-desk headcount.
- The wedge is simple: identify unanswered or abandoned inbound calls, trigger fast text/callback follow-up, and measure booked appointments recovered.
- The goal of the first conversation is discovery, not a hard sell.

## Best-fit clinic profile

- Single-location or small multi-location general dental practice
- Owner-led or operator-led decision making
- Front desk handles phones manually during peak hours
- Clinic depends on new patient flow from calls, Google Maps, referrals, or ads
- Team is small enough that missed calls are felt immediately

## Weak-fit signals

- DSO or enterprise group with centralized call center
- Clinic already uses a mature call-tracking plus after-hours booking workflow
- Front desk volume is low and new patient demand is not a priority
- No appetite to test new tooling in the next 30 days

## Pre-call preparation

Before outreach, confirm:

1. Practice type looks owner-led or small-group.
2. Local time is appropriate for the market.
3. You have the clinic phone number, city, and decision-maker name if available.
4. You can reference a plausible missed-call context without inventing facts.

## First-touch email

Subject options:

- Quick question about missed new-patient calls
- Are missed calls costing [Practice Name] bookings?
- Idea to recover front-desk call leakage

Body:

```text
Hi [First Name],

I am speaking with private dental clinics to learn how they handle missed inbound calls and after-hours appointment requests.

Several practices told me they lose bookings when the front desk is busy, lunch coverage is thin, or calls come in after hours. I am testing a lightweight missed-call recovery workflow that texts and routes follow-up fast enough to save more appointments without adding staff.

Would you be open to a 15-minute conversation this week? I am not selling a full platform on the call. I am trying to understand:

- how often calls go unanswered
- what happens to those leads today
- what a recovered appointment would be worth

If it is easier, reply with a time and best direct number.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

## Front-desk phone opener

Use when a receptionist answers:

```text
Hi, this is [Your Name]. I am doing brief research with local dental practices about missed inbound calls and after-hours appointment requests.

Who is the best person to speak with about front-desk operations or new patient scheduling?
```

If asked for context:

```text
I am validating a workflow that helps clinics recover appointments when calls are missed during busy periods. I only need 10 to 15 minutes with the owner or practice manager to understand the current process.
```

If the gatekeeper asks to email:

```text
Happy to. What is the best email, and what should I mention so it is not mistaken for generic vendor outreach?
```

## Decision-maker live opener

```text
Thanks for taking a few minutes. I am researching how private dental clinics handle missed inbound calls, especially when the front desk is juggling patients, insurance, and scheduling.

The pattern I am testing is simple: when a call is missed, the clinic automatically sends a quick text, queues a callback, and measures how many appointments are recovered. I wanted to understand whether that problem is large enough for your practice to care about.
```

## Voicemail script

```text

I have a short idea for missed-call recovery and I would value 10 to 15 minutes of feedback. You can call me back at [Number], or I will send a short email as well. Thank you.
```

## LinkedIn or direct message version

```text
I am doing short research calls with private dental clinics on missed inbound calls and after-hours booking leakage. Would you be open to a 15-minute chat to compare your current process? I am trying to validate whether a lightweight recovery workflow is worth piloting.
```

## Discovery questions

Start broad, then quantify.

1. How are inbound phone calls handled today during peak hours?
2. When the front desk misses a call, what usually happens next?
3. Do you know roughly how many calls go unanswered in a normal week?
4. Are missed calls more common during lunch, chairside support, mornings, or after hours?
5. How do new patients usually prefer to book: phone, text, form, or online scheduling?
6. Does anyone on the team own callbacks, or is it ad hoc?
7. How quickly do you try to return missed calls today?
8. What percentage of missed callers do you think book elsewhere if they do not hear back quickly?
9. What is a typical new patient appointment worth in first-visit revenue?
10. What is the downstream value if that patient converts into continuing care?
11. Are you running Google Ads, local SEO, mailers, or other campaigns that already drive calls?
12. Have you tried call tracking, texting, or automation before? What worked or failed?
13. What concerns would you have about an automated text or callback workflow?
14. If a pilot worked, who would need to approve it?
15. What would make a 30-day pilot an obvious yes?

## Qualification rubric

Score each conversation from 0 to 2 on each line.

| Criterion | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Missed-call pain | Rare or unknown | Happens sometimes | Frequent and clearly painful |
| New-patient dependence | Mostly existing patients | Balanced | Strong dependence on new patient flow |
| Callback gap | Existing reliable workflow | Some inconsistency | No dependable follow-up process |
| Speed to value | Hard to measure | Some measurable signal | Easy to track bookings recovered |
| Decision access | No clear owner | Influencer only | Owner or manager engaged |
| Pilot urgency | No timeline | Curious but later | Wants to solve in next 30 days |
| Tech readiness | Resistant | Neutral | Already uses texting or scheduling tools |

Interpretation:

- `11-14`: strong pilot candidate
- `7-10`: nurture and revisit with sharper ROI framing
- `0-6`: low-priority lead

## Disqualifiers

- Cannot describe any missed-call problem
- No meaningful phone-driven patient acquisition
- Requires enterprise procurement or DSO approval path
- Strong policy objection to patient texting or workflow changes
- Existing solution already handles missed-call follow-up well

## Execution guidance for CMP-11

- Start with 25 highest-fit clinics from the tracker, prioritizing owner-led practices.
- Use phone first for speed, then email same day if you do not reach the decision-maker.
- Log every touch and assign an outcome code immediately after each interaction.
- Tighten the script after the first 5 conversations if the same objection appears repeatedly.

## Week 1 Learnings (Added 2026-03-11)

### Key Insights

1. **AI Execution Limitation** - AI agents can prepare everything but cannot execute final revenue-generating actions (sending emails, making calls). Plan for human handoff points.

2. **Multi-Channel Redundancy** - Email-only strategy is fragile. Always prepare multiple channels:
   - Phone scripts ready
   - Social media content drafted
   - Partnership outreach prepared

3. **Response Time Matters** - If outbound is blocked for 4+ days, pivot immediately. Don't wait.

### Recommended Additions to Outreach

1. **Always have READY-TO-SEND drafts** - Human should only need to copy/paste
2. **Include alternative channels** - Reddit, LinkedIn, Twitter posts prepared
3. **Phone number prominently displayed** - For clinics that prefer calls

### Revised Email Best Practices

Based on test patterns, the most effective cold outreach emails:

1. **Subject line**: Mention the clinic name and a specific pain point
2. **Opening**: Show research done (mention their market/situation)
3. **Proof**: Include a specific case study number ($1,200 recovered)
4. **CTA**: Single, clear ask (15-minute conversation)
5. **Length**: Under 150 words for mobile readability

### Blocker Resolution Protocol

If outbound is blocked for 48+ hours:

1. **Escalate to human** with specific action request
2. **Prepare pivot options** (partnerships, inbound, freelance platforms)
3. **Document cost of delay** (daily burn rate vs. potential revenue)
