### Break-Even Analysis

**Assumptions:**
- Time to deliver pilot: ~4-6 hours setup + 2 hours/week oversight = ~14 hours total
- Hourly rate for profitability: $50/hour minimum
- Break-even hourly rate: $297 / 14 = ~$21/hour (if solo execution)

**Cost Structure:**
| Cost Category | Estimated Cost | Notes |
|---------------|----------------|-------|
| Setup labor | 4-6 hours | Call log integration, template creation |
| Weekly oversight | 2 hours × 4 weeks | Reporting, adjustments |
| Support overhead | 1-2 hours/week | Ad-hoc questions |
| Infrastructure | ~$20-50/month | Call tracking, texting platform |
| **Total cost per pilot** | **$300-400** | At $25-30/hour labor |

**Margin Analysis:**
- At $297: Near break-even or slight loss if significant support needed
- At $199/month ongoing: Healthy margin if minimal support
- Key insight: Profitability depends on low-touch delivery and conversion to ongoing

**Our Position:**

### Perceived Value Indicators

**What clinics pay for:**
1. New patient acquisition cost: $50-200 per new patient from ads
2. First-visit revenue: $150-500 average
3. Lifetime patient value: $2,000-5,000 over 3-5 years
4. Lost call cost: Every missed new patient call = $150-500 immediate loss

**Value equation:**
- If 5 calls missed/week × 4 weeks = 20 missed calls
- If 40% would have booked = 8 appointments
- If 8 appointments × $200 first-visit = $1,600 in potential revenue
- Pilot cost: $297 → ROI: 5.4x if 100% recovery, 2.7x if 50% recovery

**Conclusion:** Current pricing is defensible if we can demonstrate even modest recovery rates.

## 2. Alternative Pricing Models to Test

### Model A: Performance-Based (Pay Per Recovered Appointment)

**Structure:**
- Setup fee: $97 (covers initial configuration)
- Per recovered appointment: $50-75
- Monthly cap: $300 (after 4-6 appointments, no additional charge)

**Pros:**
- Removes risk for buyer
- Aligns our incentives with customer outcomes
- Easy to justify: "You only pay when it works"

**Cons:**
- Variable revenue, harder to forecast
- Requires robust attribution methodology
- May attract less committed customers

**When to test:**
- Strong objection to flat-fee pricing
- Need to prove value before commitment
- Early-stage trust building

---

### Model B: Tiered Pricing (Basic/Pro/Premium)

| Tier | Price | Features |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Basic** | $197 pilot / $99/month | Call detection, text-back, basic reports |
| **Pro** (current) | $297 pilot / $199/month | + Custom templates, callback queue, ROI report |
| **Premium** | $497 pilot / $349/month | + After-hours, multi-location, dedicated support |

**Pros:**
- Captures different budget levels
- Creates upsell path
- "Good, better, best" psychology

**Cons:**
- May cannibalize Pro tier
- More complexity in delivery
- Requires feature differentiation

**When to test:**
- Hearing "too expensive" from smaller practices
- Seeing interest from larger practices wanting more
- Need to capture broader market

---

### Model C: Annual Commitment Discount

**Structure:**
- Pay annual: 2 months free (10 months for price of 12)
- Pilot + 12 months: $2,297 (saves $594 vs month-to-month)

**Pros:**
- Higher initial revenue
- Locks in customers
- Reduces churn

**Cons:**
- Higher barrier to entry
- May scare off risk-averse buyers
- Requires strong trust already established

**When to test:**
- After first 3-5 successful pilots
- When customers express satisfaction
- To accelerate cash flow

---

### Model D: Freemium / Self-Service Entry

**Structure:**
- Free: DIY kit with templates and checklist
- Guided Pilot: $197 (hands-on support)
- Full Service: $297+ (we do everything)

**Pros:**
- Top of funnel for price-sensitive leads
- Builds trust before purchase
- Reduces support load for self-sufficient customers

**Cons:**
- Free tier may never convert
- Support burden for non-paying users
- Dilutes premium perception

**When to test:**
- Inbound interest from DIY types
- Content marketing driving traffic
- Need to scale without proportional headcount

## 3. Test Framework

### Pricing Test Signals

**Signs price is TOO HIGH:**
| Signal | Threshold |
|--------|-----------|
| "That's more than I expected" | 2+ times in 10 conversations |
| Price is primary objection | 3+ times after discovery |
| Ghosting after quote sent | 50%+ no response after quote |
| Requests for discounts | 2+ unsolicited in first 10 |
| "We'll think about it" | 4+ times with no follow-up |

**Signs price is TOO LOW:**
| Signal | Threshold |
|--------|-----------|
| Immediate yes without negotiation | 3+ in first 10 pitches |
| "That's it?" reaction | 2+ times |
| No budget questions | Less than 20% ask about payment terms |
| Requesting more than offered | 2+ ask "what else do I get?" |

**Signs pricing is ABOUT RIGHT:**
| Signal | Threshold |
|--------|-----------|
| Some negotiation but closes | 30-50% conversion from qualified |
| Mix of immediate acceptance and discussion | Balanced reaction spread |
| Value questions (not price) | Most questions about outcomes, not cost |

### Minimum Viable Conversion Rate

| Pricing Model | Target Conversion (from qualified lead) |
|---------------|----------------------------------------|
| Current flat-fee | 20-30% |
| Performance-based | 40-50% (lower risk = higher conversion) |
| Tiered (Pro tier) | 25-35% |
| Annual commitment | 15-25% (higher commitment barrier) |

**Decision rule:** If conversion rate is below target after 15-20 qualified conversations, consider adjusting.

### When to Test Alternative Pricing

**Trigger conditions:**
1. After 15-20 qualified conversations with current pricing
2. Consistent objection pattern emerges
3. Conversion rate outside target range

**Test protocol:**
1. Document current results baseline
2. Choose one alternative model
3. Run for 15-20 conversations
4. Compare conversion rates
5. Statistical significance: need 10+ closes per model to compare

## 4. Decision Criteria

### Pricing is Working - Continue

**Indicators:**
- [ ] Conversion rate 20-30% from qualified leads
- [ ] Objections are about timing/trust, not price
- [ ] Customers referring others without discount requests
- [ ] Pilots converting to ongoing at 60%+ rate
- [ ] Profit margin above 30% per pilot

**Action:** Stay the course, optimize delivery efficiency

### Pricing Needs Adjustment - Test Alternative

**Indicators:**
- [ ] Conversion rate below 15% from qualified leads
- [ ] Price objection in >50% of conversations
- [ ] Consistent requests for discounts or payment plans
- [ ] Competitors winning on price
- [ ] Taking >5 follow-ups to close

**Action:** Test performance-based or tiered pricing

### Pricing is Too Low - Raise Prices

**Indicators:**
- [ ] Conversion rate above 40% from qualified leads
- [ ] Almost no price objections
- [ ] Customers surprised by low cost
- [ ] Overwhelmed with delivery capacity
- [ ] Losing money on support time

**Action:** Increase pilot to $397 or add premium tier

## 7. Conversation Scripts for Pricing Objections

### "That sounds expensive for something I'm not sure will work"

**Response:**
> "I understand the hesitation. Let me put this in context: If you recover just 2 new patient appointments during the pilot, that's $400-600 in first-visit revenue. The pilot costs $297, so you're already ahead. And if for any reason we don't recover at least 3 appointments, you don't pay for month two. How does that risk-reward sound?"

### "Can you do better on the price?"

**Response (option 1 - hold firm):**
> "The price reflects the hands-on setup and support you get. Most practices see ROI within the first week. I'd rather deliver great results at a fair price than cut corners and risk your success. Would you like to hear about our pilot-to-ongoing guarantee?"

**Response (option 2 - offer value add):**
> "I can't reduce the price, but I can include an extra template revision and extend your weekly reports through month two at no extra charge. That way you get more value while we prove the model works."

### "We don't have budget for this right now"

**Response:**
> "That's exactly why this pilot is designed to pay for itself. You're not spending new budget—you're recovering revenue that's already being lost. Can we estimate your missed calls per week and see what recovery would look like?"
