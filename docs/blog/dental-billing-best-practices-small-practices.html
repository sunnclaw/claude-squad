Dental Billing Best Practices for Small Practices: A Complete Guide

Published: March 11, 2026 · Reading Time: 10 minutes

Small dental practices often struggle with billing efficiency. Without dedicated billing staff, billing can consume valuable time and create cash flow problems. This guide covers the essential best practices to streamline your billing process.

Daily Billing Processes

1. Same-Day Claim Submission

Don't let claims pile up. Submit insurance claims the same day services are rendered:

  • Reduces days in A/R
  • Improves cash flow predictability
  • Decreases chance of lost charges
  • Makes documentation fresh and accurate

2. End-of-Day Reconciliation

Before closing each day:

  • Verify all procedures were charged
  • Confirm payments were posted correctly
  • Review adjustments and write-offs
  • Balance day sheet against deposits

3. Real-Time Eligibility Checks

Verify insurance eligibility before the patient arrives:

  • Prevents surprise coverage issues
  • Allows time to collect accurate insurance info
  • Reduces claim denials

Weekly Billing Processes

1. A/R Aging Review

Every week, review your accounts receivable aging report:

Age Bucket Target % Action Required
0-30 days 70-80% Normal follow-up
31-60 days 10-15% Priority attention
61-90 days 5-8% Immediate action
90+ days <5% Collection or write-off

2. Denial Management

Set aside dedicated time each week to work denied claims:

  • Categorize denials by reason
  • Correct and resubmit within payer deadlines
  • Appeal when appropriate
  • Track denial patterns for systemic fixes

Monthly Billing Processes

1. Financial Reporting

Generate and review these key reports:

  • Production report: Total services rendered
  • Collection report: Total payments received
  • Adjustment report: Write-offs and discounts
  • Collection ratio: Should be 95%+ of production

2. Insurance Payment Analysis

Review fee schedules and reimbursement rates:

  • Compare allowed amounts to your fees
  • Identify procedures where you're losing money
  • Consider dropping low-paying plans
  • Negotiate fee increases when possible

Patient Collections Best Practices

Key Rule: Collect at Time of Service

The best time to collect patient portions is when the patient is in the chair. After they leave, collection rates drop dramatically:

  • At time of service: 95% collection rate
  • After first statement: 70% collection rate
  • After second statement: 40% collection rate
  • After 90 days: 15% collection rate

Payment Policy Best Practices

  • Clearly communicate payment expectations upfront
  • Offer payment plans for large balances
  • Accept multiple payment methods (credit cards, HSA/FSA)
  • Have patients sign financial agreements

Coding Accuracy

Common Coding Mistakes

  • Undercoding: Missing billable procedures
  • Overcoding: Billing for services not performed
  • Unbundling: Separating procedures that should be bundled
  • Wrong codes: Using outdated or incorrect codes

Coding Best Practices

  • Use current CDT code books annually
  • Train staff on new codes each year
  • Document thoroughly to support codes
  • Audit coding accuracy quarterly

Technology Recommendations

Small practices should leverage technology to compensate for limited staff:

  • Practice management software: Dentrix, Eaglesoft, or cloud alternatives
  • Clearinghouse: For faster claim submission and tracking
  • Online payments: Patient portals for easy payment
  • Automated reminders: For patient balances

Key Performance Benchmarks

Industry Benchmarks for Small Practices

  • Collection ratio: 95%+ of net production
  • Days in A/R: Under 35 days
  • Over 90 A/R: Less than 15% of total A/R
  • Denial rate: Under 10%
  • Adjustment ratio: 30-35% of gross production
