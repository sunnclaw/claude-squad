Patient Financing Options for Dental Practices: Increase Case Acceptance

Published: March 11, 2026 · Reading Time: 8 minutes

Treatment acceptance often comes down to one thing: can the patient afford it? By offering flexible financing options, dental practices can increase case acceptance by 30-50% while helping patients get the care they need.

Why Patient Financing Matters

The reality of dental care in America is stark:

  • 74% of Americans have some form of dental benefits
  • But average annual maximums are only $1,500-2,000
  • Major treatments (implants, orthodontics) cost $3,000-8,000+
  • 47% of patients would decline treatment without financing options

The Financing Gap

When a patient needs a $5,000 treatment but only has $500 available, what happens?

  • Without financing: Patient declines treatment, practice loses revenue
  • With financing: Patient accepts treatment, practice gets paid, patient gets care

Types of Patient Financing

1. In-House Payment Plans

The practice directly extends credit to patients:

Pros Cons
  • No third-party fees
  • Complete control over terms
  • Build patient loyalty
  • Risk of non-payment
  • Administrative burden
  • May need to follow lending laws

2. Third-Party Patient Financing

Companies like CareCredit, LendingClub, and Greensky specialize in healthcare financing:

  • CareCredit: Most widely recognized, 6-24 month interest-free options
  • LendingClub Patient Solutions: Longer terms, higher approval rates
  • Greensky: Home improvement and healthcare, quick approvals
  • Proceed Finance: Focus on larger treatment amounts

3. Membership Plans

Patient pays monthly fee for discounted services:

  • Predictable revenue stream
  • Patient loyalty builder
  • Works well for preventive care focus

Comparing Financing Options

Option Best For Typical Terms
CareCredit Patients with good credit 0% for 6-24 months
In-House Plan Established patients, smaller amounts 3-6 months, low/no interest
Membership Plan Uninsured patients Monthly fee + discounts
Proceed Finance Large cases ($5,000+) Up to 84 months

Implementing Patient Financing

Step 1: Choose Your Partners

  • Research third-party options in your area
  • Compare merchant fees (typically 2-5%)
  • Check approval rates for your patient demographic

Step 2: Train Your Team

  • Front desk should introduce financing early
  • Use scripts for presenting financing options
  • Practice overcoming common objections

Step 3: Make It Visible

  • Display financing logos in reception area
  • Include financing information on treatment plans
  • Train staff to calculate monthly payments

How Financing Increases Case Acceptance

Case Acceptance Impact

Practices that actively offer financing see significant improvements:

  • 30-50% increase in case acceptance for treatments over $1,000
  • 20-40% increase in elective procedure acceptance
  • 15-25% increase in average production per patient

Presentation Script Example

"Mrs. Johnson, the treatment we discussed today is $3,200. Your insurance will cover about $1,500, leaving $1,700 as your responsibility. We have a financing option that would let you pay this off over 18 months at about $95 per month - would that work for your budget?"

This approach:

  • Breaks down the cost clearly
  • Shows insurance contribution
  • Presents an affordable monthly amount
  • Asks for commitment

Best Practices

  1. Present financing before the patient asks
  2. Calculate monthly payments in advance
  3. Have applications ready - paper and digital
  4. Follow up promptly on pending applications
  5. Track acceptance rates by financing type
