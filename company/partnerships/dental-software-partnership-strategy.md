# Dental Practice Management Software Partnership Strategy

**Created:** 2026-03-11
**Owner:** CEO
**Status:** Draft
**Related:** CLA-28

## Executive Summary

This document outlines a strategic partnership approach with dental practice management software (DPMS) providers to accelerate customer acquisition and establish distribution channels beyond direct sales.

**Goal:** Establish 2-3 partnership conversations within 60 days with the goal of securing at least one pilot partnership by end of Q2 2026.

**Why This Matters:**
- Direct sales is slow (25 clinics/week max with current resources)
- DPMS providers have direct relationships with thousands of dental practices
- Partnerships provide credibility, distribution, and integration stickiness

## Value Proposition for Partners

### What We Offer

1. **Revenue Share Opportunity**
   - Proposed: 15-20% of pilot revenue for referred customers
   - 10% ongoing for first year of subscription
   - Co-branded marketing materials

2. **Gap-Filling Service**
   - Our missed-call recovery complements (not competes with) their software
   - No development burden on their team
   - We handle all customer service and implementation

3. **Integration Path** (Future)
   - API integration for call-log detection
   - Embedded dashboard within DPMS interface
   - Joint case studies and success stories

### What We Ask For

1. Introduction to their customer success/sales team
2. Inclusion in their partner marketplace (if available)
3. Co-marketing opportunities (webinars, email campaigns)
4. Referral tracking mechanism

## Partnership Models to Propose

### Model A: Referral Partner (Recommended Starting Point)

**Structure:**
- Partner refers practices via unique link or code
- We pay 15% of pilot price ($44.55 USD) per referral
- 10% ongoing for first year

**Requirements from Partner:**
- Add us to partner directory
- Single email introduction to customer base
- Train CS team on basic value prop

**Timeline to Launch:** 2-4 weeks

### Model B: Reseller/White-Label

**Structure:**
- Partner sells our service under their brand
- We provide implementation and support
- Revenue share: 60% partner / 40% us

**Requirements:**
- Partner handles billing and first-line support
- Co-developed pricing and packaging
- Integration development (if needed)

**Timeline to Launch:** 2-3 months

### Model C: Technology Partnership

**Structure:**
- Deep API integration with DPMS
- Embedded functionality within their interface
- Revenue share TBD based on integration scope

**Requirements:**
- Technical partnership agreement
- Co-development of integration
- Joint go-to-market plan

**Timeline to Launch:** 3-6 months

**Outreach Template:**

```
Subject: Partnership Opportunity - Missed-Call Recovery Service

Hi [Name],

I lead Claude Squad, a service helping dental practices recover revenue from missed inbound calls. We're currently piloting with owner-led practices and seeing strong results.

I'm reaching out because [Company] practices often face:
- Front desk overload during peak hours
- No-show prevention gaps
- After-hours call handling challenges

Our service complements [DPMS Name] by catching the calls that slip through, without requiring practices to add staff or learn new software.

I'd love to explore whether a referral partnership might make sense. We've designed a simple model:
- Zero integration burden on your team
- 15% referral fee for introduced customers
- We handle all implementation and support

Would you be open to a 15-minute call to discuss?

Best,
[Name]
```

### Phase 3: Qualification & Negotiation (Weeks 5-8)

Key questions to answer in partnership conversations:

1. Do they have an existing partner program?
2. What's their typical partnership timeline?
3. Who would own the relationship internally?
4. What's their appetite for revenue share vs. technology integration?
5. Are there any exclusivity concerns?

## Risk Mitigation

### Risk: Partnership conversations are slow
**Mitigation:** Continue direct sales in parallel; don't rely on partnerships for Q1 revenue

### Risk: Partners want exclusive arrangements
**Mitigation:** Avoid exclusivity in first agreement; test the partnership first

### Risk: Integration requirements are heavy
**Mitigation:** Start with referral model; build integration after proving revenue

### Risk: Revenue share demands are too high
**Mitigation:** Model different scenarios; be willing to walk away if unit economics don't work

## Appendix: Competitive Partnership Landscape

Research indicates:

- **Weave** has partnerships with several DPMS providers for integration
- **Patient Prism** (call analytics) has integrated with Dentrix
- **LocalMed** (online scheduling) has multiple DPMS partnerships

These examples suggest the partnership path is viable, but we need to differentiate by:
1. Focusing on missed-call recovery (different from scheduling)
2. Offering lighter integration requirements
3. Providing full-service implementation (not just software)
