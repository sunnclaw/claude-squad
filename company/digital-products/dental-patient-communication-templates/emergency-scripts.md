# Emergency Communication Scripts

## For Phone and Text Communication

---

## 1. Phone Triage: Toothache

**Use when:** Patient calls with tooth pain

**Script:**
> "I'm sorry to hear you're in pain. Let me ask a few questions to help you quickly.
>
> On a scale of 1-10, how severe is the pain?
>
> Is the pain constant or does it come and go?
>
> Is there any swelling in your face or gums?
>
> Are you having any fever or feeling unwell?
>
> [Based on answers:]
>
> - **Severe pain + swelling/fever:** This sounds like it needs immediate attention. Can you come in today at [TIME]?
> - **Moderate pain, no swelling:** Let's get you in within the next 24-48 hours. I have [TIME] tomorrow, or [TIME] the next day.
> - **Mild pain, intermittent:** Let's schedule you for an exam. In the meantime, over-the-counter ibuprofen can help with the discomfort."

---

## 2. Phone Triage: Knocked-Out Tooth

**Use when:** Patient reports avulsed tooth (emergency!)

**Script:**
> "This is a dental emergency. Time is critical - you have about 30 minutes to save the tooth.
>
> Here's what to do right now:
>
> 1. Find the tooth - pick it up by the CROWN (the white part), not the root
> 2. If it's dirty, gently rinse it with milk or water - don't scrub
> 3. If possible, gently place it back in the socket and bite down on gauze
> 4. If you can't put it back, keep it moist in milk or saliva
> 5. Get to our office immediately at [ADDRESS] or call [EMERGENCY NUMBER]
>
> How soon can you get here?"

---

## 3. Phone Triage: Broken Tooth

**Use when:** Patient reports cracked or broken tooth

**Script:**
> "I'm sorry that happened. Let me ask a few questions:
>
> Is there any pain or sensitivity?
>
> Is the broken piece sharp or irritating your tongue?
>
> Is there any bleeding?
>
> [Assess urgency:]
>
> - **Severe pain or bleeding:** This needs immediate attention. Can you come in today?
> - **Sharp edge but no pain:** We should see you within 24 hours. Avoid chewing on that side until then.
> - **Minor chip, no sensitivity:** Let's schedule you within the next few days.
>
> If you have the broken piece, bring it with you in a small container with water or milk."

---

## 4. Phone Triage: Swelling/Infection

**Use when:** Patient reports facial swelling (potential emergency!)

**Script:**
> "Swelling can be serious. Let me ask some questions:
>
> Where exactly is the swelling?
>
> How long has it been swollen?
>
> Do you have any fever or difficulty swallowing or breathing?
>
> **If difficulty breathing or swallowing:** This is a medical emergency. Please go to the nearest emergency room immediately or call 911.
>
> **If fever + rapidly growing swelling:** You need to be seen today. Can you come in at [TIME]?
>
> **If mild swelling, no fever:** Let's schedule you for today or tomorrow. In the meantime, cold compresses can help reduce swelling. Do NOT apply heat."

## 5. After-Hours Emergency Text

**Use for:** Missed emergency calls after hours

```
[PRACTICE NAME] Emergency Response:

If you're experiencing a DENTAL EMERGENCY, call our emergency line now: [NUMBER]

What counts as an emergency:
• Severe toothache
• Knocked-out tooth
• Broken tooth with pain
• Facial swelling
• Uncontrolled bleeding

If it's NOT an emergency:
• We'll call you back when we open at [TIME]
• Book online: [LINK]

For life-threatening emergencies, call 911.
```

## 6. Post-Emergency Follow-Up

**Use for:** Day after emergency appointment

**Channel:** SMS or Phone

```
Hi [PATIENT NAME], this is [PRACTICE NAME] following up on your emergency visit yesterday.

How are you feeling today?

Remember:
• Take medications as prescribed
• [SPECIFIC CARE INSTRUCTION]
• Avoid [ACTIVITY] for [TIMEFRAME]

If you have worsening pain, swelling, or fever, call us immediately at [PHONE].

Your follow-up appointment is scheduled for [DATE] at [TIME].

Take care!
```

## 7. Same-Day Appointment Request

**Use for:** Patient needs to be seen today

**Phone Script:**
> "Let me check our schedule for today...
>
> I can fit you in at [TIME] with [DOCTOR]. Does that work for you?
>
> Please arrive 10 minutes early.
>
> If you're in pain now, over-the-counter ibuprofen (if you can take it) can help until your appointment.
>
> See you at [TIME]."

## Decision Tree: Schedule Emergency or Next Day?

| Symptoms | Schedule |
|----------|----------|
| Severe pain + swelling + fever | TODAY - Emergency slot |
| Knocked-out tooth | IMMEDIATE - Drop everything |
| Broken tooth + severe pain | TODAY - Urgent care |
| Broken tooth + no pain | Within 48 hours |
| Lost filling + sensitivity | Within 24-48 hours |
| Mild toothache | Within 1 week |
| Bleeding after procedure | TODAY - Call immediately |
| Facial swelling affecting breathing | 911 - Medical emergency |

---

## Best Practices

1. **Stay calm** - Your calm tone helps anxious patients
2. **Ask specific questions** - Get the information you need
3. **Give clear instructions** - Patients are often stressed
4. **Document everything** - Note symptoms and advice given
5. **Follow up** - Check on patients the next day

---

## Red Flags (Refer to ER)

- Difficulty breathing
- Difficulty swallowing
- Swelling affecting vision
- High fever (>101°F) with dental symptoms
- Uncontrolled bleeding
- Signs of sepsis
